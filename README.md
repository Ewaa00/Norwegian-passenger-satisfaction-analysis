#Airline Passenger Satisfaction Analysis & Retention Strategy
An interactive data analytics project tailored to optimize passenger retention, identify operational thresholds, and maximize revenue stability for commercial aviation (with a focus on Norwegian Air's business model).
##Project Overview
This project analyzes 156k+ passenger records to uncover hidden patterns driving customer dissatisfaction, loyalty, and churn risk. By transforming raw operational and experiential data into actionable business strategies, this dashboard serves as a decision-making tool for flight operations, marketing, and product development teams.

##Key Business Insights
The 15-Minute Operational Threshold: Flight delays immediately trigger a drop in satisfaction. However, the steepest decline occurs within the first 15 minutes (dropping from 62% to 58%). Operational management must prioritize keeping delays strictly under this 15-minute threshold to protect brand equity.
The Premium Eco Growth Opportunity: Interestingly, Eco Plus passengers demonstrate the highest loyalty rate at 90%, outperforming even Business Class (85%). Expanding and aggressively marketing this hybrid segment offers a low-cost, high-return path to customer retention.
Digital Experience Gap: Inflight Wi-Fi and the online booking process scored the lowest ratings (averaging 2.39–2.89). In highly digitized regions like Scandinavia, addressing this "digital gap" is critical to converting dissatisfied tech-savvy Millennials and Gen Z travelers.
Revenue at Churn Risk: The High Risk segment consists of a massive volume of historically loyal customers who are currently reporting poor experiences. Implementing automated recovery campaigns (e.g., immediate digital vouchers upon a 15+ min delay) can significantly minimize churn.

##Actionable Recommendations for Norwegian Air
Optimize Buffer Management: Focus strict operational resources on mitigating minor delays (<15 mins) where customer satisfaction is still salvageable.
Upgrade Fleet Wi-Fi: Address the lowest-performing touchpoint (Inflight Wi-Fi) to recover satisfaction among younger demographic segments (18–34).
Targeted High-Risk Campaigns: Deploy automated marketing retention workflows specifically for the identified "High Risk" loyalty segment before they switch to competitors.
